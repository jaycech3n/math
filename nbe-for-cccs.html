Normalization by evaluation for CCCs

In a cartesian closed category $\mathcal C$ we can interpret the simply typed $\lambda$-calculus thus:

  • Contexts $\Gamma$ and types $A$ are presented by objects . The empty context is presented by the terminal object $\mathbf 1$.

  • Context extension $(\Gamma, x \colon A)$ is presented by . A context is hence presented by any product of the presentations of its types.

  • Terms-in-context $(\Gamma \vdash a \colon A)$ are presented by sections of the projection , or equivalently, by arrows (the maps $\pi_2 \circ -$ and are inverse isomorphisms between sections of $\pi_1$ and ), so we (ab)use the same notation for either of these presentations.

  • Substitution in terms is presented by composition of the appropriate arrows. Let $(\Gamma \vdash a \colon A)$ and $(\Gamma, x \colon A \vdash b \colon B)$ be terms in context.

    1. If these are presented as sections then $(\Gamma \vdash b[a/x] \colon B)$ may also be presented as the section
    2. If they are instead presented by the second projections then $(\Gamma \vdash b[a/x] \colon B)$ is presented by . This is just the second projection of the previous presentation (1).
